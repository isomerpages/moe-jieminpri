<p>At Jiemin Primary, National Education (NE) is an integral part of our
Character and Citizenship Education (CCE). We believe in nurturing students
who understand their identity as Singaporeans and who are equipped with
the knowledge, values, and confidence to contribute meaningfully to our
nation.</p>
<p>Through engaging and authentic experiences, our students explore Singapore’s
rich history, appreciate the strength of our multicultural heritage, and
reflect on the shared values that bind us together. These experiences include
interactive CCE lessons, meaningful NE Commemorative Events, thought-provoking
Social Studies discussions, and collaborative Cohort Learning Journeys.
These platforms encourage students to make connections between classroom
learning and real-world contexts, deepening their understanding of Singapore’s
journey and the challenges we face as a nation.</p>
<p>Opportunities such as &nbsp;outdoor learning experiences, and school-wide
events further support the development of important life skills such as
&nbsp;critical thinking, communication, collaboration, and empathy. As
students reflect on Singapore’s past and envision our future, they grow
into confident, adaptable individuals with a strong sense of purpose and
belonging.</p>
<p>Ultimately, we aim to develop active and caring citizens who are ready
to shape a better tomorrow for themselves, their communities, and Singapore.</p>
<p><strong>Strengthening Citizenship Dispositions:</strong>
</p>
<p>Our <strong>NE</strong> programmes is intentionally designed to cultivate
key citizenship dispositions in our students:</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>A Sense of Belonging:</strong> We
aim to nurture a deep understanding of our shared identity and the values
that bind us as a nation.</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>A Sense of Reality:</strong> We
encourage students to be aware of the contemporary realities, including
Singapore's unique context, challenges, and vulnerabilities in an ever-changing
world.</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>A Sense of Hope:</strong> We
strive to instill confidence and optimism in Singapore's future and build
the resilience needed to overcome challenges.</p>
<p>·&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; <strong>The Will to Act:</strong> We
aim to develop active citizens who feel a collective responsibility and
are motivated to contribute towards building a better Singapore for all.</p>
<h4><strong>Total Defence Day (TDD)</strong></h4>
<p>Total Defence Day (TDD) holds a meaningful place in the hearts of our
students and staff at Jiemin Primary School. It is a time for us to reflect
on the fall of Singapore in 1942 and, more importantly, to understand how
each of us can contribute to protecting our nation in today’s context.
At Jiemin, we believe in nurturing resilient, responsible, and united citizens.
Through engaging activities, our students learn about the six pillars of
Total Defence – military, civil, economic, social, digital, and psychological.
These lessons go beyond the classroom, helping our students understand
that every Singaporean has a role to play in safeguarding our nation.</p>
<p>From interactive scenarios to classroom discussions, our commemoration
of TDD encourages students to think critically about real-world challenges
such as pandemics, cyber threats, and misinformation. More importantly,
it fosters a deep sense of unity, national pride, and readiness to face
the future together.</p>
<p>By helping students see the relevance of Total Defence in their daily
lives, Jiemin Primary School aims to empower them to become active contributors
to Singapore’s safety, strength, and shared future.</p>

<h4><strong>Racial Harmony Day</strong></h4>
<p>Racial Harmony Day is a meaningful and cherished occasion at Jiemin Primary
School. Commemorated annually on 21 July, it marks the anniversary of the
1964 racial riots and serves as a powerful reminder of the importance of
maintaining peace and unity in our multicultural society.</p>
<p>At Jiemin, Racial Harmony Day goes beyond celebration. It is a time for
reflection, learning, and connection. Our students are immersed in activities
that promote appreciation and respect for Singapore’s cultural diversity.
These include traditional games, costume showcases, interactive booths,
and storytelling sessions that bring to life the unique customs of our
major ethnic groups.</p>
<p>Through these experiences, our students learn that harmony does not happen
by chance. It takes empathy, understanding, and collaboration. Racial Harmony
Day is not just a one-day event at Jiemin Primary. It is part of our ongoing
commitment to nurturing students who will contribute to a cohesive and
inclusive Singapore.</p>

<h4><strong>National Day Celebrations</strong></h4>
<p>National Day is a meaningful occasion for our students and staff to come
together as one school community to celebrate Singapore’s independence.
Held annually on 9 August, this commemoration marks our nation's emergence
as a sovereign country in 1965 and serves as a reminder of the strength,
resilience, and unity that define us as Singaporeans.</p>
<p>The celebration begins with a solemn flag-raising ceremony, where students
sing the national anthem and recite the pledge. This symbolic moment reinforces
a sense of loyalty and national pride, reminding everyone of our shared
identity and commitment to the nation.</p>
<p>Following the ceremony, students are treated to a vibrant National Day
concert featuring student-led performances that showcase Singapore’s cultural
diversity and community spirit. These performances reflect the creativity
and talent of our students while fostering a deeper appreciation for our
shared heritage.</p>
<p>Our students will also be engaged in meaningful classroom activities that
give them a deeper appreciation of Singapore’s journey and the values that
have shaped our nation. Through reflections, discussions and creative tasks,
they explore our country's rich heritage and think about their role in
building a better future.</p>
<p>National Day at Jiemin is not only a celebration of the past, but also
a call to the next generation to carry Singapore’s story forward with pride
and purpose.</p>
<h4><strong>NE Show Experience</strong></h4>
<p>The National Education (NE) Show is a meaningful highlight of our Primary
5 students’ learning journey. Held in July each year, it offers our students
a memorable opportunity to witness the full-dress rehearsal of the National
Day Parade (NDP), igniting a strong sense of national pride and unity.</p>
<p>From the moment our students gather in school, dressed in red and brimming
with excitement, the day is filled with anticipation. After a safety briefing
and the collection of goodie bags, they set off with their teachers and
friends, ready to experience one of Singapore’s most iconic celebrations.</p>
<p>At the Padang, they are immersed in a vibrant atmosphere. From energetic
performances and marching contingents to the spectacular sight of the Red
Lions and dazzling fireworks, the experience is truly unforgettable. The
students sing along to familiar songs, cheer with joy, and wave their flags
proudly. It was a moving experience that helped them understand the strength
of community and the pride of being Singaporean.</p>
<p>Beyond the celebrations, the NE Show offered our students a deeper appreciation
of Singapore’s journey, from past struggles to present successes, and the
values that have shaped who we are today. Through this experience, they
gained insights into the resilience, unity, and shared responsibility that
define our nation.</p>
<p>It is our hope that this special event will continue to inspire our students
to carry forward the spirit of Singapore with commitment and confidence.
More than just a celebration, the NE Show has become a cherished milestone
in their Primary 5 year, one that will stay in their hearts for a long
time to come.</p>

<h4><strong>NE Ambassadors</strong></h4>
<p>Our National Education (NE) Ambassadors play a vital role in promoting
active citizenship and national pride within the school community. As positive
role models, they inspire their peers through a range of meaningful contributions.</p>
<p>Their responsibilities include planning and leading NE-related projects,
encouraging peer participation, and supporting the successful implementation

values, and national identity to life for their fellow students.</p>
<p>Through these experiences, NE Ambassadors grow as confident leaders who
embody the spirit of service, resilience, and unity.</p>
